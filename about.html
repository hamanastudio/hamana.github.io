Yu Hamana is a founder and Creative Director.

Yu Hamana’s latest collection transforms military wear through the lens of Japanese animism, blending functionality with spiritual storytelling. Raised in Tokyo, Hamana grew up witnessing the coexistence of tradition and modernity, feeling the slow erosion of Japan’s cultural identity. To counter this, he delves into animism—the belief that spirits inhabit all things—finding connections between ancient mythology, postwar Japanese pop culture, and his own fashion practice. Inspired by his father’s affinity for functional military-style clothing, Hamana deconstructs and reinterprets military aesthetics, infusing them with nature’s organic forms. His designs reference ghillie suits, where textiles mimic the lush landscapes of Japan, creating a visual symbiosis between war’s utilitarian garments and the mysticism of the natural world. Hamana redefines military wear as a prayer for peace, reshaping its narrative through craftsmanship, traditional dyeing techniques, and modern fabrication methods.

Yu Hamana was born in Tokyo, Japan. After specialising in fine art, I worked at Kaikai Kiki Co. Ltd by Takashi Murakami, studied fashion design at the Professional Institute of International Fashion in Japan and graduated from Central Saint Martins MA Fashion 2025.
